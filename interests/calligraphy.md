My preferred pen is the Pilot Parallel, with a 1.5mm nib.

My preferred ink is the venerable Indian schoolchild ink, the Pilot V5 Hi-Tecpoint ink in red, blue, black, and green. Yes, a common gel pen ink refill fits exactly inside the same brand's distinct calligraphy pen that isn't even marketed in India.

I use 250gsm paper when I can, and usually practice on random thin notebook paper.


I like the broad-edge calligraphy style, influenced by Latin-european Blackletter orthography. I try to evolve my own shapes/approaches to letter forms parallel to taking inspiration by copying old hand-scribed Bibles, Latin-script texts, etc from Wikipedia or "gothic" style reference images and prints in media. As a side effect of practicing this specific style of calligraphy, my normal handwriting now carries the same ornateness and "swish". But the characteristics of a ballpoint/0.7mm gel pen are different from a big broad-edge, so the same muscle memory applied to the wrong pen creates a very unique style. 

I'm interested in learning about the world's other broad-edge calligraphic traditions like Islamic, Tibetan, or if there are any more outside of these.
